EXPEDITIONS
NETHORM

Nethorm is an ice giant planet in a thinly populated solar system with only nine other planets. It is about 3.4 times bigger than Earth and its gravity is about 4.49 times that of Earth. A single day lasts 37.40 hours and a year lasts 210 days. The planet is made up of 13 continents, which make up 57% of the planet's landmass and the rest are frozen oceans. Extreme weather conditions and violent tectonic plate movement make this planet unsuitable for any life. But this planet is still fairly young, so perhaps once it has aged and calmed down a bit, life might find its way to this world.

LILLES E7O

The planet LILLES E70 is an earth-like planet in a fairly small solar system with five other planets. It is about 2 times bigger than Earth and its gravity is about 2.07 times that of Earth. A single day lasts 47 hours and a year lasts 122 days. The planet is made up of 10 continents, which make up 84% of the planet's landmass. 3 moons orbit the planet and LILLES E70 itself orbits a red sun in an elliptic orbit. Resources are pretty much the only thing this planet is good for. Life will most likely never be sustained due to the harsh conditions and terra forming would be very difficult.

CECREORUS

The planet Cecreorus, which is a name still disputed, is a desert planet in a fairly large solar system with sixteen other planets. It is about 9.9 times bigger than Earth and its gravity is about 6.04 times that of Earth. The conditions on this planet may not be ideal, but they're good enough to sustain life. Unfortunately it has yet to do so, but the future of this planet is very promising. If nothing else, it would be perfect as a new home for an advanced species. 2 moons orbit the planet and Cecreorus itself orbits a yellow sun in a fairly circular orbit.

ACHERON

Formerly known as LV-426, it is one of three known moons orbiting Calpamos in the Zeta2 Reticuli system, 39 light years away from Earth. The moon was given its common name by the early human colonists who settled there. The main colonist base, Hadley's Hope, housed 158 people. Acheron has an equatorial diameter of just 1,200 km. Despite its small size, it maintains a surface gravity equivalent to 0.86 of that on Earth, indicating it is largely composed of incredibly dense materials. Its rotational period is about 2 hours. The moon's crust is made up of aluminum silicates, although there is evidence of magnesium silicate intrusion, manifested on the surface as basalt, rhyolite and microgranite lava flows.
